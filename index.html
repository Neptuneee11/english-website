Standard of Living

PDLs in the Philippines experience a low standard of living while in custody. They receive inadequate food treatment (see Iloilo District Jail) of only ₱70 per day for 3 meals. Their prisons are critically and chronically overcrowded (see Manila City Jail) and unsanitary (see New Bilibid Prison) which is further exacerbated by the insufficient training of underpaid prison personnels. This then manifests itself as the occurrence of ill-treatment by prison staff (see PNP Laguna Provincial Intelligence Facility) and poor medical care for PDLs with special needs like those with mental illness, physically disabled, young, old, and/or with sickness. PDLs also experience a lack of rehabilitation programs in their penal institutions.
