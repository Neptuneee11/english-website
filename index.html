Causes and Effects

Knowing the current state of the prison system, it makes sense to question why these things came to be and how it affects PDLs even after their stay. Admittedly, the Philippines justice system is a largely fractured and inefficient system. This makes the entire due process longer and leads to prolonged detention without any charges or trials happening which is a violation of Article 9 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights. Corruption and bribery are also rampant within the system. This then causes the people working within to be mired with an inability to meet UN standards for custodial and correctional facilities. Furthermore, this inefficient system causes PDLs' communication skills to deteriorate due to decreased social interactions and lack of rehabilitative programs. PDLs also suffer educational shortcomings and persisting stigma against them that wrongly denies them of potential opportunities, like employment after their release.
